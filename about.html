ABOUT

Asim Serdar Yilmaz: two decades inside regulated technology businesses

I am Asim Serdar Yilmaz, a senior in-house legal leader based in Istanbul. Over more than 20 years I have advised global technology, consumer and regulated businesses across Turkey and the Middle East, in retail and marketplace, streaming and media, advertising, payments, data and IP. My work tends to sit where a business wants to move quickly and a regulator wants to move carefully.

I started in private practice at White & Case in Ankara, on cross-border M&A, privatisations and IP in regulated sectors. From there I moved in-house: first into a strict, high-scrutiny compliance environment at Philip Morris International across the Middle East, then into e-commerce at eBay's Turkey and MENA business, and from 2019 to 2026 as Legal Director for Amazon Turkey. In 2026 I moved from legal into regulatory affairs, and today I lead the regulatory agenda within the corporate affairs function of a global group in a highly regulated industry.

The thread through all of it is regulated markets. I have spent years turning regulatory ambiguity into paths a business can actually take, and building what I think of as "yes, if" guardrails: controls that let a team move at speed while protecting the enterprise over the long term. Much of that has meant sitting across the table from competition authorities, data protection regulators, media regulators and ministries.

The other current chapter is LEXSAS, the AI legal-tech venture I founded in 2026, focused on legal workflow automation and advisory. It blends what the career built in layers: legal directorship, regulatory affairs, corporate affairs and public policy, now paired with hands-on AI governance. It grew out of years of building generative AI tooling for legal work, on AWS Bedrock among other platforms, and of seeing where adoption succeeds and where it quietly fails. This site is where I write about that intersection of law, regulation and AI, as informational consultancy rather than legal representation.
