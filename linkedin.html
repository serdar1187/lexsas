Notes from my LinkedIn feed

I post short observations on AI policy, legal tech and regulated digital markets. The latest one is below; the rest live on LinkedIn.

Asim Serdar Yilmaz July 2026

On June 12, the AI sitting inside a lot of everyday workflows just stopped working.

On June 26, OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6 Sol to a small group of trusted partners. OpenAI stated: "We don't believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default."

On June 12, 2026, Anthropic removed access to its frontier AI models Fable 5 and Mythos 5. Its statement: "We are complying with the government's legal directive and are removing access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all users." Export controls were lifted on July 1.

Epictetus sorted things into what we control and what we don't. I quote that line more often than I live by it. June was the opening scene where the camera lingers on a pistol for a moment, then moves on. We all know that pistol goes off before the credits.

My read: AI bans are joining a sanctions toolkit the world already knows. A year or two from now, a cutoff would not play out like frozen offshore accounts. It could bring ordinary life close to a standstill. An explicit AI policy and open-source fallbacks are the hedge. The next episode will show who prepared.
